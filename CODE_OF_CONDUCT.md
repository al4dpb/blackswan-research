# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This is a research community. We hold each other to two things:

1. **Honesty about evidence.** Overclaiming, hiding sources, plagiarizing, fabricating data, or laundering fringe claims as established science are all incompatible with participation here. So is dismissing well-sourced contested research because it's uncomfortable.

2. **Respect for the people.** No harassment, no doxxing, no slurs, no threats, no aggression directed at contributors. Disagreement is welcome; contempt is not.

## Scope

This applies to all repository spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions, commit messages, and any synchronous events organized under the project's name.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may, at their sole discretion:

- Edit or remove comments, commits, issues, and PRs that violate this code.
- Block contributors who repeatedly violate the standard.
- Decline to merge work submitted in bad faith.

There is no appeals process. If you believe a maintainer has acted unfairly, you may fork the project — this is one of the freedoms CC BY-SA 4.0 protects.

## Reporting

Email `venus@blackswanp.com` with subject line `CoC: <issue or PR URL>` and a description. Reports are read by the founder.
